DUNE Cold Electronics Technical Note
Cold Front-End ASIC Overview
Document: DUNE-CE-TN-001

1. Introduction

The DUNE Far Detector employs liquid-argon time-projection chambers (LArTPCs) operating at 87 K.
All front-end electronics are immersed in the cryostat and must function reliably at cryogenic
temperatures. The cold electronics (CE) chain consists of three custom ASICs: LArASIC, COLDADC,
and COLDATA.

2. LArASIC

LArASIC is a 16-channel, low-noise preamplifier and shaper. Each channel provides:
  - Continuous variable gain: 4.7, 7.8, 14, or 25 mV/fC
  - Peaking time: 0.5, 1, 2, or 3 µs (selectable)
  - Equivalent noise charge (ENC): < 400 e- at 150 pF detector capacitance
  - On-chip 12-bit ADC for monitoring bias and temperature

The preamplifier uses a charge-sensitive architecture with a PMOS input transistor optimized
for operation at 77–89 K. A baseline restoration circuit prevents saturation from accumulated
leakage current.

3. COLDADC

COLDADC is a 16-channel, 12-bit successive-approximation ADC running at 2 MS/s per channel.
Key specifications:
  - ENOB: 11.0 bits at 200 kHz input frequency
  - Power consumption: 1.2 mW per channel at 1.8 V supply
  - Input range: 0.2–1.6 V differential

Each COLDADC digitizes the shaped output of one LArASIC and transmits data over a low-voltage
differential signalling (LVDS) link to the COLDATA aggregator.

4. COLDATA

COLDATA aggregates serial data from eight LArASIC–COLDADC pairs into a single 1.28 Gbps
optical uplink. It distributes a 62.5 MHz clock and synchronisation commands to all front-end
chips on the same cold motherboard (FEMB).

Functions include:
  - Clock distribution and phase alignment
  - Configuration and monitoring via an I2C slow-control bus
  - Error detection and correction on the uplink

5. Front-End Motherboard (FEMB)

One FEMB carries eight LArASIC chips, eight COLDADC chips, and one COLDATA chip, reading
128 channels. FEMBs connect to wire planes via flexible cables (SHV and signal). Each APA
(Anode Plane Assembly) supports 20 FEMBs covering 2560 wire channels.

6. Wire Bias Voltages

Collection wires: +820 V
First induction wires: −370 V
Second induction wires: 0 V (grounded)
Grid wires: −665 V

Bias voltages are supplied by the warm bias voltage board (BVB) located outside the cryostat.
Stability requirements: ±1 V drift over 24 hours.

7. Acceptance Testing Procedure

Each FEMB undergoes the following tests before installation:
  a) Room-temperature function check: verify I2C communication, clock lock, data link integrity.
  b) Cold dip test: immerse in LN2 for 15 minutes; repeat function check while cold.
  c) Noise measurement: inject a known charge and verify ENC < 500 e- per channel.
  d) Crosstalk measurement: drive one channel and verify coupling < 0.5% on adjacent channels.
  e) Gain and peaking-time calibration: record per-channel gain map; flag channels > ±10% of nominal.

Boards failing any criterion are quarantined and logged in the QC database.

8. Known Issues and Mitigations

Issue: COLDADC dynamic range saturation at low peaking times with 25 mV/fC gain.
Mitigation: Restrict gain–peaking-time combinations per gain-setting table in the configuration register.

Issue: LArASIC baseline shift (< 5 mV) after rapid temperature cycling.
Mitigation: Allow 10-minute thermal stabilisation before noise measurements.
